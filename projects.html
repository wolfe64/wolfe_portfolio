Illegal Wildlife Trade Dashboard

Description

This dashboard illustrates illegal wildlife trafficking using four Tableau visualizations: a proportional dot map, a horizontal stacked bar chart, an anomaly detection graph, and a cycle plot. Filters allow exploration by taxa groups like amphibians and mammals. Key patterns, such as country-specific preferences and biome hotspots, are visible.

Analysis and Reflection

This dashboard is effective and interactive. Filters and animation help users engage with the issue. A key takeaway is how countries vary in taxa demand. The stacked bar chart shows tropical biomes experience more trafficking. The cycle plot, however, was difficult to interpret and could use improvement.

National Parks Recreational Visits: 2023

Description

This dashboard explores and ranks National Parks based on their 2023 recreational visit numbers. It includes a proportional dot map, a stacked bar chart, a tree map by region, and a line graph showing growth over time. Data was cleaned using Excel and QGIS for coordinate mapping.

Analysis and Reflection

This was an enjoyable project due to my love for National Parks. Despite simple visuals, the data preparation was extensive. One notable insight: Great Smoky Mountains National Park accounted for 14.39% of all visits, and 83% of visits in the Southeast region, likely due to its location and nearby tourist attractions.
